WHY DID WE GET INTO THIS

Our project aims to create an early warning system for landslides by utilizing past landslide data and rainfall information. We believe that this project is crucial for the safety of individuals and communities living in areas prone to landslides. Landslides can cause devastating consequences, and early detection is key to minimizing the damage they can cause. By developing this system, we hope to provide a valuable tool for individuals and organizations working to mitigate the risks associated with landslides. Our team is passionate about using technology to improve safety and protect lives, and we believe that this project is an important step towards achieving that goal.




REAL WORLD EXAMPLES

Here are a few real-world examples of landslides in Sri Lanka where there was no prediction beforehand:
Aranayake Landslide (2016): In May 2016, a massive landslide occurred in the village of Aranayake in Kegalle district, killing over 100 people and leaving hundreds homeless. The disaster was caused by heavy rains, but there were no landslide predictions beforehand.

Koslanda Landslide (2014): In October 2014, a massive landslide occurred in the village of Koslanda in Badulla district, killing over 30 people and leaving dozens missing. There were no landslide predictions made beforehand, and the disaster was triggered by heavy rains.

These examples illustrate the need for early warning systems and predictive models for landslides in Sri Lanka. By using past landslide and rainfall data, it may be possible to develop models that can predict when and where landslides are likely to occur, and take measures to prevent or mitigate their impact.

HOW PREDICTION WORKS

The code is a Flask API endpoint that receives a POST or GET request with integer features. These features are converted to a numpy array, which is passed to a pre-trained machine learning model for landslide prediction. The model predicts the probability of a landslide occurring in the given area, and if the probability is greater than 0.5, the code returns landslie warning.
